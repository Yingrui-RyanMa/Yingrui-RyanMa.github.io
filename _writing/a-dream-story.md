## The Greatest (from the night of 2025.11.08)

Sometimes I feel nervous, when it comes to battlefield. Especially nervous about all the trainings I had are going to be nothing, after about 0.3s when a bullet goes in my head. It's almost humiliating to admit that you were trained for 15 years and lost in 0.3s. It's also a dangerous thing once you have that fear. You just cannot get over it. You are too afraid that actually happens. The feeling keeps coming and coming, every time you are getting ready to hold a gun. Or in my case, energizing my arms. Apart from all that fear, every time when I start to use my power of my arms, I would feel free. It's like, I want to use it, no matter there is a war or not. It's still a mystery, what my power could do, or what consequences I have to take. I know I can move things, fly by counter-forcing the ground with all my strengths, generating a protection shield around myself, and I am also ready to explore more. However, the rules in the troop are that I could only use it when necessary. That's why I carry a gun, as the normal.

Kiran and May are a sweet couple. They are too peaceful to be working in the army. May always told me about where they are going to have their greatest wedding -- in front of an ancient tomb, would be better if it is a lost emperor's tomb. The location would not be open air, just in front of the coffin deep underground. She said it's not because she wants the dead to witness, but she wants someone aged, breathing or not. I don't remember the face of her boyfriend. Maybe it's just too long ago, and the fact that he does not interest me. Maybe he does not interest May either.

Chris is a librarian. I don't know why he is here but he always carries a book, a thick closed book with dark green covers and extremely white page edges. He also has glasses on which makes him more bookish. He has a son I never met. He joins the military to get his son into a nice local school.

There is another colleague. I never know his name because for one thing, he won't tell me. For another, I don't care. All I can remember now is that he is black, wearing sunglasses all the time. He's kind of like a movie character, but I don't know which character exactly, maybe he is just too professionally immersed to leave me such an impression.

Our base is in a desert, at least that's how I see it. There are two main sites, the headquarter, where the couple and Chris work, and a two-floor factory with complex structures. I have absolutely no idea what our mission was, just to protect it from other humans or maybe more. In the headquarter, there is a monster, we fed it with electricity and kept it as our weapon for future conflicts.

There is a factory we have been safeguarding for a while. Me and that colleague with no name. I will just call him Agent from now on. Agent and I were at the second floor of the factory when a group of enemies arrived silently. The invasion started with an electrical bomb that destroyed our headquarter, which we can see from our point of view. The whole air was full of electrical lightnings and dusts from the sand. I quickly run towards the headquarter, deftly avoiding the lightning while trying to find the rest of the crew.

The lightning moved between the sand and the sky until there was nothing left of the headquarter except a crater and a smell I could not name. It smells pretty good actually, nothing like I would imagine on the battlefield. I ran toward it anyway because that's what you do.

Chris was sitting at the crater's rim with his book open. I have never seen it open before. The pages were the same extreme white, but they were all blank. He seemed satisfied with this and did not look up when I arrived.

I found May and Kiran together. Kiran was laying on his back. May was holding his hand, facing away from me. "The monster went out." I did not ask about Kiran because she did not explain, and I did not care.

Agent appeared beside me and took off his sunglasses for the first time. His eyes were ordinary. He asked me to go after the monster. I asked why me. He said because you know what it feels like. Then he put his sunglasses back on.

I rose up into the air with the strength of my arms by counter-forcing the ground and move closer to the monster. I looked at that pile of machine and did not react with anything. Just staring at it while the monster paused. I held my shield open, around both of us, like the way you hold a coat open for someone standing in the cold, and I waited. Eventually, I made it to the ground, and the monster calmed and stopped.

Later we all sit outside the factory to watch the sunset. At some point, May said "I still want the wedding underground". But nobody responded to this. It was not the kind of thing that needed a response anyway. Both of my arms are still shaking, like activated in some way when I woke up.
