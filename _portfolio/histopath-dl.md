## Deep Learning for Histopathology

This project focuses on developing and applying deep learning methods to analyse histopathology whole-slide images (WSIs) for cancer research. By leveraging convolutional neural networks and vision transformers, we aim to improve the accuracy and efficiency of pathological diagnosis.

### Key Areas

- **Whole-slide image classification** — Predicting tumour subtypes and grades from gigapixel pathology images
- **Cell and tissue segmentation** — Automated delineation of biologically relevant structures
- **Biomarker prediction** — Inferring molecular markers directly from H&E stained slides

### Methods

We employ multiple instance learning (MIL) frameworks, attention-based aggregation, and self-supervised pre-training to handle the unique challenges of computational pathology.
